Bankroll strategies

Pick one style and use its numbers in the ladder’s settings. Each plan suits different risk comfort and progress wishes.

Aggressive higher risk

For confident, high-volume winners comfortable with swings.
Pro: Fastest climb to bigger stakes.
Con: Frequent move-downs, large fluctuations possible.
  • 12 BI, 2-shot ladder — Shot start: 12 BI, shot size: 2 BI, move down: 10 BI.
  • Takes shots early for rapid growth, but with minimal buffer for variance.

Balanced popular

For most winning players who want reliable progress with manageable swings.
Pro: Good compromise of stability and speed.
Con: Progress may be slower than aggressive options.
  • 20 BI, 3‑shot ladder — Shot start: 20 BI, shot size: 3 BI, move down: 17 BI.
  • Bigger cushion and shot size, avoids yo-yo swings while still moving up efficiently.

Conservative lowest risk

For new/rebuilding players or anyone wanting maximum safety.
Pro: Extremely rare to run out of shots, very stable.
Con: Slow climb, may feel “stuck” at stakes for longer.
  • 30 BI, 3‑shot ladder — Shot start: 30 BI, shot size: 3 BI, move down: 27 BI.
  • Large buffer before shots, rarely drop stakes, prioritize security over speed.
