PLO bankroll strategies

PLO's higher variance requires significantly larger bankrolls than NLH. Pick a conservative approach that matches your risk tolerance and can handle 25+ buy-in swings.

Aggressive higher risk

For highly skilled PLO players with proven edges and high volume who can handle massive swings.
Pro: Faster progression through stakes despite high variance.
Con: Extreme bankroll swings, frequent move-downs during bad runs.
  • 40 BI, 10-shot ladder: Shot start 40 BI, shot size 10 BI, move down 30 BI.
  • Minimum viable for skilled players, but expect 20-30 BI swings regularly.

Balanced recommended

For consistent PLO winners who want steady progression with manageable swings.
Pro: Good balance of safety and growth, handles most variance.
Con: Still significant swings compared to NLH strategies.
  • 80 BI, 15-shot ladder: Shot start 80 BI, shot size 15 BI, move down 65 BI.
  • Solid middle ground that can weather typical PLO variance while progressing.

Conservative safest

For PLO beginners, professionals, or anyone who wants maximum protection from variance.
Pro: Nearly bulletproof against downswings, minimal risk of going broke.
Con: Very slow progression, requires patience and large starting bankrolls.
  • 150 BI, 25-shot ladder: Shot start 150 BI, shot size 25 BI, move down 125 BI.
  • Professional-level safety for those who cannot afford to rebuild bankrolls.
