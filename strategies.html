Bankroll strategies

Compare popular cash-game bankroll approaches and pick a plan that matches your risk tolerance, skill edge, and goals. Each preset can be used directly in the ladder settings.

Aggressive presets higher risk

Who it suits: Confident winners with strong edges who play high volume and are comfortable with swings.
Pro: Fastest climb to higher stakes.
Con: More frequent move-downs and bigger bankroll swings.

12 BI, 2-shot ladder

  • Shot start: 12 BI; Shot size: 2 BI; Move down: 10 BI (derived)
  • The most aggressive common approach—quick testing of higher limits with minimal cushion

15 BI, 2-shot ladder

  • Shot start: 15 BI; Shot size: 2 BI; Move down: 13 BI
  • Slightly more conservative than 12 BI while maintaining aggressive shot-taking speed

Balanced presets popular

Who it suits: Solid players who want fewer move-downs while still progressing steadily.
Pro: Good balance between safety and progression speed.
Con: Slower climb compared to aggressive approaches.

20 BI, 3‑shot ladder

  • Shot start: 20 BI; Shot size: 3 BI; Move down: 17 BI
  • Popular middle-ground approach with larger shot sizes for extended testing periods

25 BI, 2‑shot ladder

  • Shot start: 25 BI; Shot size: 2 BI; Move down: 23 BI
  • Conservative shot-taking with smaller risk per attempt and larger safety cushions

Conservative presets lowest risk

Who it suits: New players, bankroll rebuilders, or anyone with low variance tolerance.
Pro: Maximum stability and time to adapt at each level.
Con: Slowest progression; may feel "stuck" at levels for long periods.

30 BI, 3‑shot ladder

  • Shot start: 30 BI; Shot size: 3 BI; Move down: 27 BI
  • Large cushions with substantial shot budgets for thorough limit testing

Classic cushion (no shots)

  • Operate at 30-40+ BI for current stake; move up only when full buffer is achieved
  • Traditional approach without shot-taking—move up only with complete bankroll for next level
