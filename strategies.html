Poker Bankroll Ladder

Compare popular cash-game bankroll approaches and pick a plan that matches your risk tolerance, skill edge, and goals. Each preset can be used directly in the ladder settings.

Aggressive presets higher risk

12 BI, 2-shot ladder

  • Shot start: 12 BI; Shot size: 2 BI; Move down: 10 BI (derived)
  • Who it suits: confident winners with a strong edge who play lots of hands and are comfortable with swings
  • Pros: Fastest climb, quick to test higher stakes
  • Cons: More frequent move-downs, bigger bankroll swings

15 BI, 2-shot ladder

  • Shot start: 15 BI; Shot size: 2 BI; Move down: 13 BI
  • Who it suits: proven winners who want a small cushion but value speed over maximum safety
  • Pros: Still relatively fast, but steadier than 12 BI
  • Cons: Shot-taking can still be swingy

Balanced presets popular

20 BI, 3‑shot ladder

  • Shot start: 20 BI; Shot size: 3 BI; Move down: 17 BI
  • Who it suits: grinders with solid edge who want fewer “yo-yo” move-downs
  • Pros: Fewer move-downs, balanced shot/cushion speed
  • Cons: Climb is slower than aggressive ladders

25 BI, 2‑shot ladder

  • Shot start: 25 BI; Shot size: 2 BI; Move down: 23 BI
  • Who it suits: steady players optimizing for fewer move-downs and more cushion
  • Pros: Gentler swings, more time at each limit
  • Cons: Slowest progress to the next limit among shot-takers

Conservative presets lowest risk

30 BI, 3‑shot ladder

  • Shot start: 30 BI; Shot size: 3 BI; Move down: 27 BI
  • Who it suits: newer players, those rebuilding a bankroll, anyone with a low tolerance for swings
  • Pros: Almost never bust the shot budget, plenty of time to adapt at each limit
  • Cons: Climb is slowest; players may feel “stuck” at a level

Classic cushion (no shots)

  • Operate at 30-40+ BI for the current stake, move up only when the full buffer for the next stake is achieved
  • Who it suits: very conservative or variance-sensitive players, beginners
  • Pros: Extremely stable, lowest risk of ruin
  • Cons: Slowest progress, less exposure to high-stakes play
