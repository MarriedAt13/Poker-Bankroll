Tournament bankroll strategies

Tournament poker requires massive bankrolls due to extreme variance. Even winning players can go months without significant scores. Choose a conservative approach based on your risk tolerance.

Conservative recommended

For tournament beginners, rebuilding players, or anyone who cannot afford to go broke.
Pro: Maximum protection against long downswings and variance.
Con: Requires very large bankrolls to play meaningful buy-ins.
  • 150+ buy-ins: Maintain 150-300 buy-ins for your chosen tournament level.
  • Best for large field tournaments (1000+ players) where variance is extreme.
  • Can survive 100+ buy-in downswings that are common in tournament poker.

Standard balanced

For experienced tournament players with proven results and steady bankroll growth.
Pro: Reasonable balance of risk and bankroll requirements.
Con: Still vulnerable to extended downswings without careful management.
  • 100 buy-ins: The most common professional tournament bankroll standard.
  • Works well for medium field tournaments (100-1000 players) with good ROI.
  • Assumes solid tournament skills and disciplined bankroll management.

Aggressive high risk

For highly skilled players with large sample sizes and proven positive ROI.
Pro: Allows playing higher buy-ins with smaller total bankrolls.
Con: High risk of ruin during inevitable downswings.
  • 50-75 buy-ins: Minimum viable for skilled tournament professionals.
  • Only suitable for small field tournaments (less than 100 players) or sit-and-gos.
  • Requires excellent game selection and strong mental game to survive swings.
